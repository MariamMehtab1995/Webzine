Chess and Artificial Intelligence

Chess computers started in the 1970s and evolved rapidly:

  • Deep Blue: The IBM supercomputer that defeated world champion Kasparov in 1997.
  • AlphaZero: Google’s AI learned chess by playing itself and has amazing strategic depth.
  • Today, AI helps players analyze games, develop strategies, and train better than ever.

Artificial intelligence has transformed chess, blending human creativity with computer calculation.
