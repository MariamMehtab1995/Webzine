The Legendary Origins of Chess

Chess is a timeless game, believed to have originated in ancient India around the 6th century CE — a journey that blends strategy, culture, and history.

The earliest form of chess was known as Chaturanga, symbolizing the four divisions of the Indian army:

  • Infantry (Pawn)
  • Cavalry (Knight)
  • Elephants (Bishop)
  • Chariots (Rook)

From India, the game migrated to Persia, where it became known as Shatranj. The English terms “Check” and “Checkmate” are derived from Persian phrases: Shah (meaning “king”) and Shah Mat (meaning “the king is defeated”).

By the 15th century, chess rules had evolved closer to the modern game as it spread through Europe, capturing minds and cultures worldwide.
