The Legendary Origins of Chess

Chess was invented in ancient India, around the 6th century.

The early game was called Chaturanga, meaning "four limbs" of an army:

  • Infantry (Pawn)
  • Cavalry (Knight)
  • Elephants (Bishop)
  • Chariots (Rook)

From India, it spread to Persia, where it became known as Shatranj. The words “Check” and “Checkmate” come from Persian phrases Shah (king) and Shah Mat (king defeated).

By the 15th century, chess rules evolved into the modern game during its journey to Europe.
