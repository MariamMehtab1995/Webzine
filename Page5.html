Traditional Pakistani Clothing varies widely across regions, showcasing local culture, climate, and traditions, with unique styles, fabrics, and decorations.
Gilgit-Baltistan: Pakistani Costumes showcase vibrant colors and unique patterns, with women in long skirts and men in woolen jackets to suit the colder climate.
Khyber Pakhtunkhwa: The Pashtun shalwar kameez is common, accompanied by embroidered topis for men, while women favor colorful dresses with jewelry.
Sindh: Sindhi women sport bright ghagra and choli, embellished with mirror work and embroidery. Men wear sherwani along with the traditional Ajrak shawl.
Punjab: Women typically wear shalwar kameez adorned with phulkari embroidery, while men opt for kurta and shalwar, often paired with colorful turbans.
